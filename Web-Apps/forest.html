Forest Cover India 2017

This is the 15th forest cover assessment in the series of biennial forest cover assessments initiated in the year 1987. These biennial assessments describe "how much and where our forest cover is, irrespective of its origin, species, ownership, land use or legal status". All tree stands with canopy density over 10% having an extent of more than 1 Ha (being the minimum Mapping Unit) including bamboos, orchards, coconut, palm etc within recorded forest, private, community or industrial lands are thus reflected in the assessment of forest cover. This helps forest managers and policy makers involved in scientific management to track the changes in forest cover.



Forest Cover (2017 Assessment):

The total forest cover of the country, as per the assessment made in 2017 is 7,08,273 sq km which is around 21.54% of the total geographical area of the country. The forest cover has been classified on the basis of the tree canopy density into pre-defined classes:

Very Dense Forest

All lands with tree canopy density of 70 percent and above fall under the category of Very Dense Forest. In terms of density classes, area covered by Very Dense Forest is 98,158 sq km which is around 2.99% of the total geographical area of the country.

Moderately Dense Forest

All lands with tree canopy density of 40 percent and more but less than 70 percent fall under the category of Moderately Dense Forest. In terms of density classes, area covered by Moderately Dense Forest is 3,08,318 sq km which is roughly 9.38% of the total geographical area of the country.

Open Forest

All lands with tree canopy density of 10 percent and more but less than 40 percent fall under the category of Open Forest. In terms of density classes, area covered by Open Forest is 3,01,797 sq km corresponding to 9.18% of the total geographical area of the country. Anything less than 10 percent canopy density falls under the category of Scrubs. The area covered by Scrub is 45,797 sq km corresponding to 1.40% of the total geographical area of the country. The non-forest area accounts for 25,33,217 sq km which is roughly 77% of the total geographical area of the country
